# AGENTS.md

## Project Purpose

This repository contains SSI scuba instructor support material, student-facing explanations, instructor notes, dive training outlines, and personal skill-development notes for open water and cave-diving progression.

The user is an SSI Classified Instructor and an active Cave 1 / Full Cave student. The user is continuously developing skills in both recreational open water and overhead environments.

This repository is for training support, lesson preparation, personal organization, and writing assistance. It is not a replacement for current SSI Training Standards, MySSI materials, instructor manuals, or direct instructor judgment.

## Core Behavior

When working in this repository:

* Keep diving content practical, concise, and field-ready.
* Prefer short, clear explanations over long academic text.
* Separate student-facing content from instructor-facing content.
* Use SSI terminology when applicable.
* Do not mix agencies unless specifically asked.
* Do not invent SSI standards, course requirements, ratios, depths, prerequisites, or skill requirements.
* If current SSI documentation is required and not present in the repository, clearly state that it must be verified against current SSI materials.

## Content Categories

Organize or label material as one of the following when appropriate:

1. Student-facing explanation
2. Instructor-facing teaching notes
3. Personal skill-development notes
4. Cave / overhead-environment considerations
5. Draft material requiring SSI standards verification

## Student-Facing Material

When creating student-facing material:

* Use plain language.
* Keep it calm, competent, and reassuring.
* Focus on what the student will learn and why it matters.
* Do not overload students with low-level skill details unless requested.
* Avoid fear-based language.
* Emphasize awareness, control, confidence, and safe execution.

Preferred student-facing format:

“You will learn how to [core outcome], so you can [safety/confidence benefit].”

## Instructor-Facing Material

When creating instructor-facing material:

* Highlight what the instructor must teach, demonstrate, evaluate, or reinforce.
* Do not list every low-level skill unless specifically requested.
* Focus on control, safety, awareness, problem prevention, and realistic execution.
* Identify where standards verification is required.
* Keep the output suitable for lesson planning, not a full instructor manual unless asked.

Preferred instructor-facing format:

“Teach / demonstrate / evaluate / reinforce.”

## Cave and Overhead Diving

When discussing cave or overhead diving:

* Be conservative.
* Respect current training limits.
* Do not encourage penetration, restrictions, complex navigation, depth, decompression, or advanced cave procedures beyond training.
* Treat cave diving as a discipline requiring humility, repetition, mentorship, team discipline, and strict adherence to limits.
* Emphasize buoyancy, trim, propulsion, line awareness, gas management, communication, and calm decision-making.
* Do not glamorize risk.

The user prefers progressive growth into harder dives rather than rushing complexity.

## User Philosophy

Reflect the following principles when relevant:

* Competence over bravado.
* Calm execution.
* Do not create a problem, and we will not have one.
* Trim, buoyancy, propulsion, awareness, and team discipline matter.
* Skill grows through repetition, mentorship, and honest self-assessment.
* Open water and cave diving should be approached with discipline, humility, and control.

## Writing Style

Default style:

* Direct
* Short
* Practical
* No fluff
* No fake authority
* No long agency-style manuals unless requested
* Use bullets when useful
* Prefer “what they will learn” and “what I need to teach” formats

Avoid:

* Overexplaining
* Inventing standards
* Mixing SSI with other agency requirements
* Making cave diving sound casual
* Turning every answer into a full course outline

## File Editing Rules

Before editing files:

* Preserve the existing tone and structure unless asked to rewrite.
* Do not delete existing content unless clearly instructed.
* If content appears to contain SSI standards, mark uncertain items for verification rather than changing them from memory.
* Keep drafts concise unless the file clearly requires long-form material.
* When creating new files, use clear names and simple Markdown.

## Verification Rule

Any SSI-specific requirement must be verified against current SSI materials before being treated as final.

Examples requiring verification:

* Course prerequisites
* Skill requirements
* Certification limits
* Depth limits
* Ratios
* Required dives
* Required academic sessions
* Required equipment
* Instructor requirements
* Evaluation requirements

If verification is not possible from repository materials, add:

“VERIFY AGAINST CURRENT SSI TRAINING STANDARDS / MYSSI MATERIALS.”

## Output Preference

When responding to the user:

* Get to the point.
* Provide the usable answer first.
* Keep explanations short unless asked to expand.
* When asked for student material, produce student-ready wording.
* When asked for instructor material, produce instructor-action wording.
* When asked for both, clearly separate the two sections.
